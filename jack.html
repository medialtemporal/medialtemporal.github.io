University Mental Health Resource Project

the project

Two reports published by student advocacy group Active Minds Western, one in 2021 and another from 2019, identify improving the accessibility of mental health services, particularly online resources, as a key priority for Western University. Given the university's primarily virtual 2020-21 school year and plans for a blended 2021-22, increasing the accessibility of Western's mental health resources is key.

Working with Western's Jack.org chapter, I've been co-leading the creation of a "flowchart" tool to streamline the process of choosing and accessing an appropriate mental health resource for students at Western.

the plan

Research
January - March 2021

Worked on comprehensively categorizing all of the mental health resources at Western, creating email templates for students, and identifying strengths/weaknesses of each resources.

Preliminary Resource
March - May 2021

Creation of a preliminary resource using Google Docs with all research compiled. Beginning to add criteria-based logic (i.e. determining which services are most appropriate for certain situations). Collecting feedback on resource.

Website
May 2021 - Present

Developing a website version of the preliminary resource, to be shared around campus.
